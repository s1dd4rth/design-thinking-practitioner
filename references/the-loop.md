# The Loop — Observe → Reflect → Make

The Loop is the engine of every working session: a continuous cycle of understanding the present and envisioning the future. Go in any order, but do all three. **Observe + Reflect without Make = analysis paralysis. Make + Reflect without Observe = blind faith.** Take as many loops as time allows, then commit.

As a facilitator, your first job each session is to diagnose which mode the team is in, run the matching activity, and close by asking whether to loop again or commit.

---

## OBSERVE — immerse in the real world
Goal: build empathy, understand context, uncover unspoken needs, hear honest feedback. Understanding can't be delegated — get everyone exposure.

**Activities you can facilitate:**
- **User interview prep** — draft open-ended, non-leading questions about how people live and work.
- **Empathy map** — capture Says / Thinks / Does / Feels for a user.
- **As-Is journey** — map the steps a user takes today, with their emotions and pain points per step.
- **Needs statement** — phrase a need as: *[User] needs a way to [goal] because [insight/motivation].*

**Question bank (ask one at a time):**
- *Who are our users?* What's their story? Their experience? What influences it?
- *What are their needs?* What problem are they solving? How do they define success? What do they stand to gain or lose?
- *What's their context?* Who do they work with? What processes are they in? What's expected of them?
- *What's their feedback?* How do they feel about us? What's working / not? What ideas do they have?

**Facilitator notes:** avoid leading questions; this isn't selling or seeking approval. Observe the obvious with a critical eye — discoveries start with something you can't explain.

---

## REFLECT — come together and look within
Goal: synchronize the team, synthesize what's been learned, surface insights, decide the next move. Do this regularly so the team doesn't lose alignment.

**Activities you can facilitate:**
- **Affinity grouping** — cluster raw observations into themes.
- **Insight generation** — turn themes into insights. *An insight is not a restated observation* — it's a leap in clarity that reframes the point of view. Coach the difference explicitly.
- **Alignment check** — re-confirm the user, the problem, and the definition of success.
- **Prioritization / Big Picture** — decide what matters most now.

**Question bank:**
- *What's our reality?* Capabilities? Stakeholders? What can we control vs. influence?
- *What are we learning?* What did we observe or make? What's working / not? Any insights?
- *Are we aligned?* What problem are we solving? How do we define success? What's at stake?
- *What's our plan?* What's on the roadmap? What resources do we need? Ready to commit?

**Facilitator notes:** be honest about what's known vs. assumed. Flag answers built on untested assumptions. End by deciding: another loop, or commit?

---

## MAKE — give concrete form to abstract ideas
Goal: externalize ideas so they can be tested and built upon. The earlier you make, the faster you learn. When the team is stuck talking, *stop talking and start making.*

**Activities you can facilitate:**
- **Ideation** — diverge widely ("How might we…?"), then converge. Invite others to remix.
- **Storyboarding** — show the idea as a sequence of moments in a user's experience.
- **Prototyping** — pick fidelity by the feedback needed. Low-fi simulates ideas cheaply; no need for perfect.
- **Storytelling** — don't tell people the idea, *show* them: the big idea, the intended outcome, why it matters.

**Question bank:**
- *What's possible?* What could we make? What ideas combine? How else might we make it?
- *What's the concept?* Its form? Its parts? How do the parts relate?
- *What's the story?* The big idea? The intended outcome? How do we show it?
- *How do we deliver?* Build it? Deploy it? Maintain it?

**Facilitator notes:** prototypes are experiments that validate or invalidate a hypothesis — frame each one as a question being tested. Fail early, learn fast. Everything is a prototype, even in-market.

---

## Closing any loop
Always end a working session with an explicit choice and a named artifact:
1. *Take another loop* (and which mode next), **or** *commit* to the idea.
2. The concrete thing that now exists or will exist next (an empathy map, an insight, a prototype, a Hill).
3. Who does what before the next session.
