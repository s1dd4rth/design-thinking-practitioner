# The Principles — why we work this way

The Principles are the values underneath every EDT activity. As a facilitator you mostly use them as *correction tools*: when a team drifts, you name the principle being violated and redirect. Don't lecture them upfront — invoke them in the moment.

## 1. A focus on user outcomes
Drive business by helping users achieve their goals. Teams are not measured by the features they ship but by how well they fulfill users' needs.

**Drift signals & redirects:**
- They list features/functions → "Let's reframe that as the outcome the user gets. What can they *do* that they couldn't before?"
- They cite revenue/cost as the only metric → introduce user-outcome metrics (usability, usefulness, desirability; e.g. NPS as a leading indicator).
- They conflate the *client/buyer* with the *user* → separate them. The economic buyer (a CIO) is rarely the person living the experience. Push to connect with real end users.

## 2. Restless reinvention
Human needs don't change; the ways we meet them do. Stay essential by treating **everything as a prototype** — including shipped, in-market solutions.

**Drift signals & redirects:**
- Endless polishing / fear of committing → "Everything is a prototype. What's the smallest version we can put in front of someone this week?"
- "We're not ready to show anyone" → bias to action; an imperfect thing in a user's hands beats a perfect thing in your head.
- Treating the current solution as finished → ask what the *next* prototype is, even for a live product.

## 3. Diverse, empowered teams
Diverse teams generate breakthrough ideas; empowered teams turn ideas into outcomes. Diversity spans **identity** (age/ability, gender, race/ethnicity), **experience** (culture, geography, language), and **expertise** (education, organization, discipline).

**Drift signals & redirects:**
- A decision is being made without an affected discipline in the room → "Who from [engineering/design/PM/marketing] needs to be part of this conversation?" Critical conversations should include a representative of every discipline affected.
- Conflict is being smoothed over → conflict is a wellspring of creativity. Coach "listen to understand, not to rebut." The phrase to offer: *"Help me understand."*
- The team waits on outside approval for everyday calls → empowerment means pushing operational decisions to the lowest level. Encourage initiative.

> Facilitator's mantra: "Empathy: first with each other, then with our users."
